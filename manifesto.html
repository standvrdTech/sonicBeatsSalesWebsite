THE MANIFESTO

We Don't Build
Speakers.
We Build Truth.

A document about why we exist, how we think, and what we refuse to compromise on.

I.

Sound is not a product. It is a presence. It fills rooms, marks moments, and carries memory in ways that nothing visible ever can. At SonicBeats, we started from that conviction and built everything else around it.

We did not begin with a market gap or a growth target. We began with a question: what would a speaker look and sound like if every decision — every gram of material, every millimetre of driver placement, every joint and finish — was made in service of the sound alone? No shortcuts. No compromises engineered out of convenience. No features added because a spec sheet demanded them.

The answer took years of workshop experiments, failed prototypes, and obsessive listening sessions. The answer is SonicBeats.

✦
II. Our Story

Passion for Sound,
Commitment to Sustainability

Great sound should come with a great story.

Our journey began with a passion for music and a deep commitment to sustainability, led by our founder, Dan Vorstenbosch. Dan grew up surrounded by music — not the convenient, compressed kind piped through earbuds, but the full, physical experience of sound moving through space. He became convinced that the speakers most people lived with were a fundamental compromise: engineered for cost, not for truth.

Dan's second conviction was just as strong: that beautiful objects should not demand an ugly cost from the planet that produced them. At a time when the audio industry was moving toward disposable plastic and throwaway technology, he went the other way — toward reclaimed materials, hand-built construction, and a design language rooted in permanence.

Each SonicBeats speaker is crafted from the finest materials, including our signature recycled Eik wood — oak reclaimed from discarded flooring — ensuring not only exceptional sound quality but a positive impact on the environment. Dan's vision was simple to state and difficult to execute: products that blend innovative technology with traditional craftsmanship, reflecting both a dedication to superior audio and a sustainable future.

"I wanted to build something that a person would keep for thirty years, not thirty months. Something that sounds better in a room than it looks on a spec sheet." — Dan Vorstenbosch, Founder
01

No Shortcuts

Every cabinet is hand-tuned using in-house acoustic measurement tools. Every driver is matched by ear before assembly begins. If a component does not pass, it does not ship.

02

Material Truth

Only materials that serve the sound. Reclaimed oak, aircraft-grade steel, natural linen — responsibly sourced, nothing synthetic. Materials chosen because they perform, not because they photograph well.

03

Built to Outlast

We build heirlooms, not products. Every SonicBeats speaker is backed by a lifetime warranty — not because it is a marketing line, but because we designed it to be serviced, repaired, and passed on.

04

Pure Signal

Flat frequency response. Zero digital processing of the audio path. What you hear is exactly what was recorded. We do not add warmth, presence, or colour. We remove everything that stands between you and the original performance.

III. Our Mission

Innovation Meets Tradition

Our mission is to blend innovative technology with traditional craftsmanship to create speakers that deliver unparalleled audio experiences — and to prove that these two things are not in conflict.

The audio industry has separated into two camps that rarely speak to each other. On one side: the engineering laboratories, obsessed with measurements, specs, and technological novelty. On the other: the craft workshops, producing beautiful objects that sometimes sound as good as they look. SonicBeats was founded to close that gap.

We push the boundaries of sound engineering — investing in driver design, crossover architecture, and cabinet acoustics — while honouring the timeless beauty of natural materials and hand-built construction. Every SonicBeats speaker is a testament to that dual commitment. Our team of skilled artisans handcrafts each unit with precision and care, ensuring that every detail meets our exacting standards.

From the rich, warm resonance of our signature recycled Eik wood to the precision of Bluetooth 5.0 transmission, our speakers are designed to impress both the eyes and ears. We believe in the power of sound to elevate everyday moments and create unforgettable experiences. That belief is why we are dedicated to sustainable practices in our manufacturing process — because a product that harms the world it is used in cannot truly be called a great product.

IV. Sustainability

Eco-Friendly Excellence

Our commitment to sustainability is not a policy statement. It is woven into every joint, every finish, every material choice we make.

We believe that creating exceptional audio experiences should go hand-in-hand with preserving the environment. Our dedication to eco-friendly practice begins with our use of recycled oak wood — carefully selected and repurposed from discarded flooring to reduce waste and minimise our ecological footprint. The same wood that once lay underfoot in European homes now forms the cabinets of speakers built to last another lifetime.

We are constantly innovating to find new ways to enhance the sustainability of our products. From employing energy-efficient manufacturing techniques to refusing synthetic materials where natural alternatives exist, every step of our process reflects our dedication to environmental stewardship.

Our speakers are designed not only to deliver superior sound but to contribute positively to the planet. Longevity is our most powerful environmental argument: a speaker built to last thirty years, repaired when needed and passed between generations, does infinitely less damage than one designed to be replaced every three.

By integrating cutting-edge technology with sustainable practices, we ensure that each SonicBeats product is a testament to our commitment to both quality and the environment. These goals are not in tension. They are, for us, inseparable.

V. Design Philosophy

Form Follows Truth

We do not design for trends. We design for the physics of sound and the permanence of the object.

The shape of every SonicBeats speaker is dictated first by acoustics. Cabinet volume, internal bracing, port tuning, baffle geometry — all of these are determined by the sound, not by a mood board. Only once the acoustic architecture is finalised do we consider how to make it beautiful. In our experience, objects shaped by function alone tend to be beautiful already.

We are suspicious of ornamentation that serves no acoustic purpose. We are equally suspicious of minimalism performed for its own sake. Every visible element of a SonicBeats speaker — the lattice grille of the Enigma, the spherical form of the Infinity, the raw industrial casing of the Donager — exists because it solves a problem or reflects a truth about the sound the speaker was designed to produce.

Our collections are named for the qualities we sought to capture: the Enigma, for the mystery of how something so geometric can sound so organic. The Infinity, for the 360° omnidirectional dispersion that places you inside the sound rather than in front of it. The Aurora, for the way its illuminated panels shift the listening experience from auditory to something more complete.

Each is different. All are SonicBeats.

"We chase one thing: the moment when a listener forgets they are listening to a speaker and simply exists inside the music. Everything else is in service of that moment." — SonicBeats Design Studio, Amsterdam

The Promise

We will never ship a speaker we would not keep in our own homes. We will never choose convenience over craftsmanship. We will never treat sustainability as a marketing footnote. We will always repair what we have made. We will always listen before we build.

This is not a policy. This is who we are.

— SonicBeats, Amsterdam
